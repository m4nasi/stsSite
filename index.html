WHAT IS THE STUDENT TO STEMETTES PROGRAM?

Who are they?

The Student to Stemette is part of Born to Be, Deutsche Bankâ€™s youth engagement programme which helps young people to develop the skills, aspirations and access to the opportunities they need to reach their potential.

What do they aim to do?

Student to Stemette: aims to build successful and fulfilling Science, Technology, Engineering & Maths (STEM) careers for young women aged 15+, using a wealth of women in STEM industries.

Why do they bring people together?

There are a good number of reasons to stay on the STEM path; they provide the right motivators and toolsets for female students to take advantage of all that the STEM industry has to offer.

Skills you can gain from the program

These skills are how I have developed throughout the program with the help of my mentor

Mentor: someone who teaches or gives help and advice to a less experienced and often younger person.

Mentee: a person who is advised, trained, or counselled by a mentor.

What sort of things can you talk about with your mentor? Does it have to be STEM related?
Not at all! My mentor has helped me with my part time job as well as university applications!






Can you pick your mentor? What happens if you and your mentor are not a great match?
We take part in a speed networking night where you speak to all the mentors and then pick. If it isn't a match, it's not a problem you can ask and you'll be assigned to another mentor








How often (and how) do you talk with your mentor? Do you ever meet up in person?
We meet every fortnight through Microsoft Teams! Unfortunately, due to Covid-19 we were unable to meet in person but you get to know your mentor very well!
